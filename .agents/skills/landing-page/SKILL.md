# Landing Page Copywriter Guidelines

These guidelines focus on specialized, conversion-driven copy for landing pages, utilizing proven marketing frameworks and emotional triggers.

## 1. Discovery Checklist (Before Writing)

Before generating copy, identify:
- **Product/Service**: Exactly what is being sold.
- **Target Audience**: Who is the ideal customer and their specific "pain."
- **Problem**: The primary challenge the product solves.
- **Unique Advantage (USP)**: Why this solution is better than alternatives.
- **Primary Action (CTA)**: The single goal of the page.
- **Social Proof**: Data points, reviews, or existing testimonials.

---

## 2. Copywriting Frameworks

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- **Problem**: Identify the specific pain point.
- **Agitate**: Amplify the consequences of not solving it (Cost of Inaction).
- **Solution**: Present your product as the bridge to the "after" state.

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
- **Attention**: Grab them with a bold, benefit-driven headline.
- **Interest**: Build curiosity with specific details and scenarios.
- **Desire**: Create an emotional pull using benefits and transformations.
- **Action**: Direct them to a single, clear CTA.

### StoryBrand (The Hero's Journey)
- The **Customer** is the Hero (not you).
- The Hero has a **Problem**.
- Meets a **Guide** (your brand) who has a **Plan**.
- Calls them to **Action** that results in success and avoids failure.

---

## 3. High-Conversion Page Sections

### Hero Section (The 5-Second Test)
- **Headline**: Core value proposition in < 10 words.
- **Subheadline**: 1-2 sentences expanding on the "How."
- **Trust Indicators**: "Used by 1,000+ clinics" or "5.0 Rating."
- **Primary CTA**: High-contrast button with action verbs.

### Problem & Solution Sections
- **Problem**: Use emotional resonance; describe 2-3 specific "nightmare" scenarios.
- **Solution**: Explain the "Magic" behind the fix. 3-5 benefit-focused bullet points.

### "How It Works" (Reducing Friction)
- 3 simple steps (Step 1, Step 2, Step 3).
- Focus on how easy it is to start.

### FAQ & Objections
- Address 5-7 common hesitations directly (Price, Time, Setup complexity).
- Answers should be confident and risk-reversing.

---

## 4. Writing & CTA Best Practices

- **Active Voice**: Use present tense and strong verbs.
- **"You" Language**: Focus 100% on the customer's world.
- **Scannability**: Short sentences, plenty of white space, and bold headers.
- **CTA Formula**: `[Action Verb] + [What They Get]`.
    - **Prefer**: "Start My Free Audit" over "Contact Us."
    - **Note**: Using first-person ("My") often increases conversion.
