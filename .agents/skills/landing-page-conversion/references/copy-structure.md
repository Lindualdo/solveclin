# Conversion Copy Structure

Copywriting formulas, section-by-section copy guidelines, and psychological triggers for high-conversion landing pages.

---

## The Master Formula: AIDA + PAS Hybrid

**A**ttention → **I**nterest → **D**esire → **A**ction  
layered with  
**P**roblem → **A**gitate → **S**olve

Use AIDA for the macro flow. Use PAS within sections that need persuasion (Problem, Features, Testimonials).

### StoryBrand (complementar ao AIDA + PAS)
Quando a oferta envolve transformação pessoal ou serviço de confiança, use a lente StoryBrand:
- O **Cliente é o Herói** — nunca a marca
- O Herói tem um **Problema**
- Encontra um **Guia** (a sua marca) com um **Plano claro**
- O Guia faz uma **Chamada à Ação** que leva ao sucesso — e evita o fracasso

> Aplicação prática: o copy nunca deve colocar a empresa como protagonista. *"Ajudamos clínicas a crescer"* (cliente é o herói) > *"Somos especialistas em automação"* (marca como protagonista).

## Section-by-Section Copy Guide

### 1. NAV
**Goal**: Orient + prime for conversion

- Logo (left)
- Max 4 nav links — use benefit-oriented names ("Pricing" not "Plans")
- CTA button: short, action-driven ("Get Started", "Book a Call", "Try Free")
- Optional: small badge or announcement bar above nav ("🎉 New: [Feature]")

**Copy rule**: Nav CTA must match Hero CTA exactly. Consistency reduces friction.

---

### 2. HERO
**Goal**: Stop the scroll. Communicate value. Capture a click.

**Headline formula options:**
- `[Do X] without [pain/obstacle]` → "Scale your agency without hiring more people"
- `The [adjective] way to [achieve goal]` → "The fastest way to close more deals"
- `[Number] companies use [product] to [result]` → Social proof headline
- `Finally, a [category] that [promise]` → "Finally, a CRM that doesn't suck"

**Subheadline**: 1-2 sentences. Expand the promise. Who it's for. What makes it different.
> "[Product] is [what it is] that helps [who] [do what] so they can [outcome]."

**CTA copy rules:**
- Use a verb: "Start", "Book", "Get", "Try", "See"
- Be specific about what happens: "Start Free Trial" beats "Get Started"
- **First-person possessive increases conversion**: "Start *My* Free Trial" > "Start Free Trial"; "Get *My* Custom Plan" > "Get a Plan"
- Add micro-copy under CTA: "No credit card required" / "Cancel anytime"
- Secondary CTA: "See how it works →" (watch demo or scroll)

**Hero visual**: Should reinforce the headline. Product screenshot, result, or abstract that implies speed/power/clarity.

---

### 3. SOCIAL PROOF BAR
**Goal**: Borrow authority fast

- "Trusted by teams at [logos]"
- OR metric: "Join 12,000+ businesses already growing with [Product]"
- OR review aggregate: "★★★★★ 4.9/5 from 400+ reviews"

**Copy tone**: Matter-of-fact, not boastful. Let the logos/numbers speak.

---

### 4. PROBLEM (Agitate)
**Goal**: Make them feel the pain. Create urgency.

Start with empathy. Name the frustration they feel RIGHT NOW.

Formula:
```
You're dealing with [problem].
And despite trying [solution they tried], it's still [pain].
The result? [consequence they fear].
[Product] changes that.
```

Example:
> "You're spending hours chasing leads that go nowhere. You've tried spreadsheets, other CRMs, even hiring an SDR. But your pipeline is still unpredictable. That costs you deals — and sleep. [Product] was built to fix exactly this."

**Design note**: This section often looks best as a single column, large text, dark background — editorial feel.

---

### 5. SOLUTION (Introduce offer)
**Goal**: Position your product as THE answer

- Don't list features yet — sell the transformation
- "Introducing [Product]: [one-sentence promise]"
- Show the before/after: "Before: [state A] → After: [state B]"
- Use a product visual here (screenshot, mockup, video)

**Copy rule**: Lead with outcome, support with mechanism.
> ✅ "Close 3x more deals with automated follow-ups"  
> ❌ "Our platform has automated email sequences"

---

### 6. FEATURES / HOW IT WORKS
**Goal**: Build desire. Show it's real and it works.

**How It Works (3-step process):**
Step labels should be action-oriented:
1. "Connect your tools" → "Onboard in 5 minutes"
2. "Set your goals" → "Tell us what you need"  
3. "Watch results compound" → "Growth on autopilot"

**Feature cards:**
Each feature = 1 icon + 1 benefit headline + 1-2 sentence description
- Headline: outcome first ("Never miss a follow-up")
- Description: mechanism second ("Automated reminders keep every deal moving forward")

**Copy rule**: Max 2 lines of body copy per feature card. Scannable > exhaustive.

---

### 7. SOCIAL PROOF (Testimonials)
**Goal**: Prove it works for people like them

**Testimonial formula:**
1. Before state: "I used to [pain]..."
2. After state: "Now [result]..."
3. Specific metric: "We went from X to Y"
4. Human close: "I couldn't imagine going back"

**Format:**
```
"[Quote with specific result]"
— [Full Name], [Title] at [Company]
```

**Credibility signals**: Real photo (not stock), company logo, role title, specific numbers.

**Avoid**: Vague praise ("Great product!"), no last name, no company.

---

### 8. PRICING
**Goal**: Remove the risk. Make the decision easy.

**Tier naming** (avoid Generic/Premium/Enterprise):
- Use outcome names: "Starter", "Growth", "Scale"
- Or audience names: "Solo", "Team", "Agency"

**Highlighted tier copy:**
- Add "Most Popular" badge
- Add a sentence about WHY it's popular: "Best for teams ready to scale fast"

**Per feature line:**
- List as benefits, not features: "Unlimited contacts" not "CRM module"
- Use checkmarks with specific quantities

**CTA per tier:**
- Free/low tier: "Start Free" or "Get Started"
- Mid tier: "Start Free Trial" or "Choose [Tier]"
- Enterprise: "Talk to Sales" or "Book a Demo"

**Risk reducers under CTA:**
- "30-day free trial · No credit card"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "14-day money-back guarantee"

---

### 9. FAQ
**Goal**: Handle objections before they kill the sale

Write FAQs as the actual objections, not informational questions:
- "Is [Product] right for [my situation]?"
- "What happens after my trial ends?"
- "How long does setup take?"
- "Do you integrate with [common tool]?"
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"

**Answers**: Short. Direct. 2-4 sentences max. End with a reassurance or link to more info.

---

### 10. FINAL CTA
**Goal**: Convert the people who read everything

This is for your most engaged visitors. They've read it all — now close.

Formula:
```
[Headline] — restate the core promise with urgency
[Subheadline] — address the last hesitation
[Primary CTA]
[Risk reducer]
```

Example:
> **"Ready to close more deals — without the chaos?"**  
> Join 12,000+ teams already using [Product]. Set up takes under 10 minutes.  
> [Start Your Free Trial]  
> No credit card required · Cancel anytime

## Psychological Triggers Cheatsheet

| Trigger | How to Use |
|---------|------------|
| **Social proof** | Numbers, logos, testimonials, reviews |
| **Scarcity** | "Only 3 spots left", "Early access closing soon" |
| **Urgency** | "Price increases Friday", "Limited beta access" |
| **Authority** | Press mentions, certifications, founder credibility |
| **Reciprocity** | Free resource, free trial, free audit |
| **Loss aversion** | "Every day without [product] costs you [X]" |
| **Specificity** | "Saves 4.2 hours/week" beats "Saves time" |
| **Future pacing** | "Imagine [life after product]..." |
| **Us vs Them** | "Built for [specific type], not everyone" |
| **Risk reversal** | "30-day guarantee, no questions asked" |

## Microcopy Patterns

### CTA microcopy (below button)
```
✓ Free 14-day trial   ✓ No credit card   ✓ Cancel anytime
```

### Form field labels
- Use placeholder text that shows an example: `name@company.com` not `Email Address`
- Add benefit near form: "Get your free audit in 24 hours"

### Badge/Tag copy
```
New · Just launched
#1 on Product Hunt
Used by Fortune 500 teams
SOC 2 Certified
```

### Error/empty states (conversion killers to avoid)
- Never show "Error" without saying what to do
- Empty state on pricing: show the most popular plan by default

## Headline Testing Framework

When given a choice, test this priority order:

1. **Specific outcome** — "Generate 50 qualified leads/month"
2. **Time-based promise** — "Land your first client in 7 days"
3. **Pain removal** — "Stop losing deals to slow follow-up"
4. **Social proof headline** — "How 5,000 agencies grew 3x with [X]"
5. **Curiosity gap** — "The lead generation strategy your competitors don't know"

Avoid:
- ❌ Feature-first headlines ("Introducing our new AI dashboard")
- ❌ Vague promises ("Transform your business")
- ❌ Jargon ("Leverage synergistic go-to-market strategies")
