# Marketing Psychology & Mental Models

These guidelines consolidate foundational psychology principles and mental models to influence buyer behavior and optimize marketing efficiency.

## 1. Foundational Thinking Models

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
- People "hire" products to accomplish a specific job.
- **Application**: Focus on the *outcome* (e.g., a filled surgical schedule) rather than the *process* (AI automation).

### Inversion
- Ask "What would guarantee this campaign fails?" then systematically prevent those failures.
- **Application**: Avoid confusing pricing, slow load times, and vague CTAs before trying to be "innovative."

### Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)
- 80% of results come from 20% of efforts.
- **Application**: Identify the 20% of channels or services (e.g., dental implants vs. cleanings) that drive 80% of revenue and double down on them.

## 2. Understanding Buyer Behavior

### Paradox of Choice
- Too many options lead to paralysis.
- **Application**: Limit pricing tiers to 3. Recommend a "Most Popular" or "Best for Clinics" option.

### Loss Aversion (Prospect Theory)
- Losses are twice as painful as equivalent gains.
- **Application**: Frame messaging around "Stop losing €X/month to missed calls" instead of just "Increase your revenue."

### IKEA Effect & Endowment Effect
- People value things more if they've put effort into them or "owned" them.
- **Application**: Use free trials or "Configure your agent" wizards to build investment before the sale.

## 3. Persuasion & Influence (The "Cialdini" Heuristics)

### Reciprocity
- Give value first to create a sense of obligation.
- **Application**: Offer a free "Diagnostic Strategical" or Mystery Shopping report before asking for a setup fee.

### Authority Bias
- Deferring to experts.
- **Application**: Highlight 20+ years of IT experience, certifications, and "Featured in" logos to build instant trust.

### Anchoring & Decoy Effect
- The first number seen influences all subsequent judgments.
- **Application**: Show a high-value "Premium Engineering" price first to make the "Agent Setup" look affordable. Use a decoy tier that makes the target tier look like the best value.

---

## 4. Pricing Psychology

### left-Digit Effect (Charm Pricing)
- $497 feels significantly cheaper than $500. Use .97 or .99 for value-driven offers.

### Rule of 100
- For prices < $100, use % discounts (20% off).
- For prices > $100, use absolute discounts ($100 off).

### Mental Accounting
- Frame the price within a context they already spend on.
- **Application**: "The cost of one missed implant surgery pays for the entire year of the agent."

---

## 5. Design & Conversion Models

### Hick's Law
- Decision time increases with choice complexity. Keep landing pages focused on a **single CTA**.

### BJ Fogg Behavior Model
- **Behavior = Motivation x Ability x Prompt**.
- **Application**: If a user has high motivation but the form is hard (low ability), they won't convert. Make "Ability" high by using easy chat interfaces.

### AIDA Funnel
- **Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**. Ensure the page layout follows this logical progression.
