# Copywriting Specialist Guidelines

These guidelines provide a structured framework for creating high-conversion marketing copy, focusing on clarity, customer-centricity, and logical flow.

## 1. Core Principles

### Clarity Over Cleverness
- If you have to choose between being creative or being clear, **always choose clear**.
- Avoid marketing jargon and buzzwords (e.g., "streamline", "leverage", "innovative").

### Benefits Over Features
- **Feature**: What it does (e.g., "24/7 AI Chatbot").
- **Benefit**: What that means for the customer (e.g., "Never lose a lead from a 10 PM WhatsApp message again").

### Specificity Over Vagueness
- ❌ "Save time on your workflow."
- ✅ "Cut your weekly scheduling tasks from 5 hours to 10 minutes."

### Customer Language (Voice of Customer)
- Mirror the exact words and phrases customers use in reviews, interviews, and support tickets.
- Speak *to* the customer, not *at* them.

---

## 2. Page Structure Framework

### Above the Fold (The Hook)
- **Headline**: The single most important message. Formula: `{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}`.
- **Subheadline**: Expands on the headline, adds one layer of specificity (1-2 sentences).
- **Primary CTA**: Action-oriented and value-driven (e.g., "Start Free Trial" or "Get My ROI Report").

### Core Sections
- **Problem/Pain Point**: Agitate the existing frustration.
- **Solution/Approach**: How you solve it differently.
- **Key Workflows/Benefits**: 3-4 distinct units of value.
- **Social Proof**: Testimonials, numbers, or logos.
- **Final CTA**: Remind them of the stakes and offer the next step.

---

## 3. Writing Style Rules

1.  **Simple Over Complex**: "Use" not "utilize", "help" not "facilitate".
2.  **Active Voice**: "We generate leads" not "Leads are generated".
3.  **Active Verbs**: Use strong verbs that imply action and results.
4.  **No Exclamation Points**: Let the strength of the copy create the urgency, not the punctuation.
5.  **One Idea Per Section**: Don't dilute the message by combining unrelated benefits.

---

## 4. CTA Copy Guidelines

Avoid "Weak" CTAs and use "Benefit-Driven" CTAs:
- ❌ **Weak**: Submit, Sign Up, Learn More, Click Here.
- ✅ **Strong**: Start My Free Trial, Get My Custom Plan, See Audit in Action, Download Private Checklist.

**Formula**: `[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]`

---

## 5. Page-Specific Strategy

- **Homepage**: Serve multiple audiences with the broadest value prop.
- **Landing Page**: Single message, single CTA. Pure focus on conversion.
- **Pricing Page**: Help visitors choose. Address "which plan is right for me?".
- **About Page**: Tell the "why". Connect mission to customer benefit.
