# Sales Specialist & Enablement Guidelines

These guidelines consolidate strategic sales frameworks and enablement best practices. They are designed to bridge the gap between technical solutions (like AI Agents) and business outcomes (revenue and efficiency).

## 1. Core Sales Principles

### Trust Over Features
- Use the client's language, not technical or marketing jargon.
- Involve real-world feedback in asset creation.
- If a client or sales rep has to "translate" your proposal, it has failed.

### Situation-Specific Communication
- Tailor all assets (decks, one-pagers) to the specific buyer persona (e.g., Clinical Director vs. Administrative Manager).
- Match the depth of information to the deal stage (Awareness vs. Decision).

### Scannability & Outcomes
- Reps and buyers should find answers in 3 seconds. Use bold headers and visual hierarchy.
- Every technical feature must tie back to a **Business Outcome**:
    - ❌ "AI-powered scheduling"
    - ✅ "Eliminate 80% of manual scheduling time"
    - ✅ "Recover €1,200/month in missed appointments"

---

## 2. High-Impact Pitch Deck Framework (10-12 Slides)

1.  **Current World Problem**: The specific pain the clinic faces today (e.g., missed calls, empty chairs).
2.  **Cost of Inaction**: What it costs them in Euros/Time to *not* change.
3.  **The Shift**: Technology or market changes making this the right moment (e.g., AI maturity).
4.  **The Approach**: How SolveClin solves this differently (Engineering of Processes).
5.  **Solution Walkthrough**: 3-4 key workflows (not a feature list).
6.  **Proof Points**: Logos, metrics, or authority markers.
7.  **Case Study**: One specific success story told well.
8.  **Implementation Timeline**: How they go from "Zero" to "Live".
9.  **ROI / Value Projection**: Expected return and payback period.
10. **Pricing Overview**: Transparent and tiered.
11. **Next Steps/CTA**: Clear, time-bound action.

---

## 3. Objection Handling Framework

For every objection (Price, Complexity, Trust), document:
1.  **Objection Statement**: Exactly how the client says it.
2.  **The "Why"**: The real concern (e.g., "Too expensive" often means "I don't see the value").
3.  **Response Approach**: Acknowledge, Validate, and Redirect.
4.  **Proof Point**: Specific evidence or analogy to debunk the concern.
5.  **Follow-up Question**: Re-engage the client to move forward.

---

## 4. ROI Calculator & Value Props

### Design Principles
- **Inputs**: Current state metrics provided by the clinic (e.g., leads/month, current conversion rate).
- **Calculations**: Your formula for value (e.g., `Value = (leads * improved_conversion * ticket_price) - setup_cost`).
- **Outputs**: Annual ROI %, Payback period (months), and Total 3-year value.

---

## 5. Proposal & Playbook Standards

### The "5-7 Page" Proposal
1.  **Executive Summary**: Focus on the problem, solution, and outcome (1 page).
2.  **Solution Map**: Mapped directly to their requirements.
3.  **Investment**: Transparent pricing and terms.
4.  **Call to Action**: Decision timeline.

### Strategic Playbooks
- Document buyer personas, qualification criteria (e.g., Challenger Sale/Mystery Shopping), and competitive positioning.
- Playbooks are "living documents"—review quarterly based on win/loss data.
